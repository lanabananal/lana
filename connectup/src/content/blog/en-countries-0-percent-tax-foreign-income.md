# Countries with 0% Tax on Foreign Income: How Territorial Taxation Works

Most people assume that wherever they live, they owe taxes on everything they earn, no matter where the money comes from. That assumption is wrong, and it costs a lot of people a lot of money every year.

A significant number of countries operate under what is called a territorial tax system. Under this model, only income generated within the country's borders is taxable. If you live in one of these countries and earn money from clients, investments, or businesses located outside its territory, that income is not subject to local tax.

For freelancers, remote workers, entrepreneurs, and investors with international income, this is not a loophole. It is the actual law.

## What Territorial Taxation Actually Means

The majority of high-income countries use a worldwide taxation system. Germany, France, Spain, Australia, Canada, and most of Europe tax their residents on all income, regardless of source. If you live in France and have a client in Singapore, France wants its cut.

The United States goes even further with citizenship-based taxation, meaning Americans owe US taxes no matter where they live or earn, for life.

Territorial tax systems work differently. The country only cares about economic activity that happens inside its borders. Foreign-source income, whether from overseas clients, foreign dividends, international investments, or remote work for companies outside the country, falls outside the tax net entirely.

The practical result for someone with exclusively foreign-sourced income: effective tax rate of 0%.

## Countries That Offer Territorial Taxation

Several well-known jurisdictions operate this model. Panama is often the first name that comes up. Its Friendly Nations Visa is relatively easy to obtain, and foreign income is not taxed. The downside is that Panama is increasingly expensive and has been subject to more international financial scrutiny in recent years.

Georgia has become popular among digital nomads for its low cost of living, easy residency, and a small business tax regime that can reduce rates to around 1% on income below certain thresholds.

The UAE offers 0% personal income tax across the board, but establishing genuine residency there requires meaningful presence and costs that put it out of reach for most people who are not already high earners.

Singapore and Hong Kong both use territorial systems but have become expensive and competitive hubs where residency requires significant ties to the local economy.

Paraguay stands out in this landscape for a specific combination of factors: one of the simplest residency processes in the world, among the lowest costs to establish legal residence, a clean territorial tax system backed by national law, and a cost of living that makes maintaining residency genuinely viable without a large income.

## How Paraguay's System Works

Paraguay's territorial taxation is established under Law No. 6380/19. The principle is direct: only income generated within Paraguayan territory is subject to personal income tax. Foreign-source income, regardless of amount, origin, or currency, falls completely outside the scope of taxation.

This means that a freelancer with clients in Europe, a consultant with US-based contracts, or an investor with international holdings pays 0% Paraguayan tax on all of that income, provided it is structured correctly as foreign-sourced.

If you generate income within Paraguay, that income is taxed at progressive rates between 8% and 10%, which remains one of the lowest rates in Latin America. There is no wealth tax, no inheritance tax, and no capital gains tax on foreign investments.

## The Part Most Articles Skip

Territorial taxation does not exist in a vacuum. To benefit from Paraguay's 0% rate on foreign income, you need to actually establish legal tax residency there, which requires going through the immigration process, obtaining a Paraguayan ID (cédula), and registering with the tax authority (RUC).

More importantly, you also need to properly end your tax residency in your previous country. Simply obtaining Paraguayan residency does not automatically free you from your home country's tax obligations. Most countries require a formal exit process, and some apply exit taxes on assets held at the time of departure.

Doing this correctly, in the right sequence, with proper documentation, is what determines whether the structure holds up legally. Doing it incorrectly, or doing it halfway, creates exposure in both jurisdictions.

## Who This Actually Makes Sense For

Territorial tax residency is not the right move for everyone. It makes the most sense for people who have or are building income streams that are genuinely international, have geographic flexibility in where they live, earn enough that the tax savings meaningfully outweigh the cost and effort of relocation, and are willing to genuinely restructure their life rather than just acquire a foreign document.

For someone earning exclusively from local clients in their home country with a fixed job and family obligations, it simply does not apply. For someone working remotely for international clients, running an online business, managing foreign investments, or building location-independent income, the math can be dramatic.
