## Understanding the Maquila Regime

Paraguay's Maquila Law (Law No. 1064/97) is a special economic regime that allows foreign companies to outsource part of their industrial or service process to a Paraguayan company, paying an extremely low effective tax rate in return.

It's one of the most underutilized yet powerful business structures in South America — and for international entrepreneurs, it can be transformational.

## How the Maquila Works

Under the Maquila regime:

1. A **foreign company** (the "maquiladora") enters into a contract with a Paraguayan company
2. The Paraguayan company performs **productive or service activities** on behalf of the foreign company
3. The outputs are exported back to the foreign company
4. The Paraguayan company pays only a **1% tax on the value-added** (the "maquila tax")

Yes, you read that correctly. **1% tax** on the added value — not on gross revenue.

## Who Is the Maquila For?

The Maquila regime is ideal for:

- **Software development companies** outsourcing development to Paraguay
- **Manufacturing businesses** looking for a low-cost, low-tax production base
- **Service companies** that can outsource any productive activity
- **E-commerce businesses** using Paraguay as a logistics or fulfillment hub
- **International entrepreneurs** who want a legal, tax-efficient corporate structure

## Key Benefits

### 1. Extremely Low Tax Rate
The 1% maquila tax on value-added is among the lowest effective business tax rates in the world. Compare this to 25–35% corporate tax rates in many other countries.

### 2. No Import Duties
Machinery, equipment, and raw materials brought in for maquila production enter Paraguay duty-free under a temporary admission regime.

### 3. No VAT on Exports
Goods and services exported under the maquila regime are exempt from Paraguay's 10% VAT.

### 4. Currency Freedom
No currency controls. You can hold and transact in USD, EUR, or any other currency.

### 5. Political Stability
Paraguay has maintained stable economic and political conditions, making it a reliable long-term base.

## Requirements to Set Up a Maquila Company

To operate under the Maquila Law, you need:

1. **Register a Paraguayan legal entity** (SAE, SRL, or SA)
2. **Sign a maquila contract** with the foreign principal company
3. **Register the contract** with the Consejo Nacional de las Industrias Maquiladoras de Exportación (CNIME)
4. **Maintain proper accounting** and submit annual reports

The process typically takes 2–4 months with professional assistance.

## Real Example

Imagine you run a software development agency based in Germany. You establish a Paraguayan SRL under the Maquila regime. The German company contracts the Paraguayan company to develop software. The Paraguayan company:

- Employs local developers (relatively low labor costs)
- Produces deliverables (software) exported to Germany
- Pays **only 1% tax** on the value added in Paraguay

Meanwhile, you, as the owner with Paraguay tax residency, benefit from Paraguay's territorial tax system on your personal income.

## Combining Maquila + Tax Residency

The most powerful combination for international entrepreneurs is:

1. **Paraguay tax residency** (personal tax optimization)
2. **Maquila company** (corporate tax optimization)

This dual structure, when implemented correctly and legally, creates a highly efficient tax footprint while remaining fully compliant with both Paraguayan law and international standards.
