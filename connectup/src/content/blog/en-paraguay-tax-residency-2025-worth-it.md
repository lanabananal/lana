Every year, more professionals from Brazil, Europe, and Latin America research Paraguay tax residency. The promise is compelling: **0% tax on foreign income**, a simple process, and one of the lowest costs of living in South America.

But is it actually worth it? Let's look at the real numbers, the real process, and the real risks.

## What Paraguay Tax Residency Gives You

Paraguay operates on a **territorial tax system**. That means:

- Income earned **inside Paraguay** is taxed at 10% (flat rate)
- Income earned **outside Paraguay** — including remote work for foreign companies, freelance income in foreign currency, dividends from foreign companies — is **0% taxed**
- No capital gains tax on foreign assets
- No inheritance tax on assets held outside Paraguay

For someone earning $5,000/month from international clients, that's potentially **$0 in income tax** on foreign earnings — versus anywhere from 15% to 40%+ in most countries.

## The Real Process

**Timeline: approximately 60 days**

1. **Free consultation** — understanding your profile, current tax situation, and strategy
2. **Document preparation** — typically 3–6 weeks
3. **First trip to Asunción** — approximately 5 business days (biometrics, paperwork, RUC registration)
4. **Waiting period** — typically 30 days
5. **Second trip to Asunción** — pick up your Paraguayan cedula (ID)
6. **SET certificate** — fiscal residency certificate required by banks and brokers

The process requires commitment but is not complicated — with proper guidance.

## The Risks and What They Are Not

**"It's not legal"** — False. Paraguay tax residency is a treaty-compliant, internationally recognized legal mechanism. It is used by thousands of professionals worldwide.

**"Paraguay will go after you"** — Paraguay has no income tax on foreign earnings. There is nothing to "go after."

**"Your home country will still tax you"** — This depends on your country. Brazil, for example, has a fiscal exit process. ConnectUP and its Paraguayan legal partners guide you through this step specifically. Done correctly, the fiscal exit is fully legal.

**"You have to live in Paraguay"** — No. Temporary residency requires one visit per year. Permanent residency requires one visit every 36 months.

## Who Should Do It

**Good candidates:**
- Freelancers and self-employed professionals earning $2,000+/month internationally
- Digital entrepreneurs with foreign revenue
- Remote workers employed by foreign companies

**Who might wait:**
